# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A single athlete using Garmin data for personal training analysis. The user context is frequent self-review sessions to understand recent load, long-term trends, route patterns, and period-over-period changes.

## Product Purpose

Provide a clear, reliable personal training analytics workspace that pulls Garmin activity and health data into one place so the user can quickly assess fitness direction, review training history, and compare performance across periods.

Success looks like fast answers to practical questions such as:

- "How is my fitness trending right now?"
- "What did this month/year look like compared to another?"
- "Where and how often am I training?"

## Brand Personality

Analytical, modern, clear.

Voice and tone should feel calm, precise, and practical. The product should support confident decision-making without visual noise.

## Anti-references

Do not resemble:

- Template card-grid ecommerce layouts
- Loud promotional banner/sale styling
- Overly decorative gradients or attention-seeking animation patterns

## Design Principles

1. Insight First
   Prioritize fast understanding of trends, comparisons, and training signals over decorative UI patterns.

2. Clarity Under Density
   When presenting multiple metrics and charts, preserve strong hierarchy and legibility so complex information still scans quickly.

3. Consistent Analytical Language
   Use consistent interaction and visual patterns across analysis, heatmap, training log, compare, and records surfaces so the user can stay in flow.

4. Practical Confidence
   Favor restrained, trustworthy presentation that feels modern but never promotional or theatrical.

5. Progress Over Hype
   Support regular training reflection and decision-making with grounded signals, not marketing-style urgency.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No dedicated accessibility scope has been requested for this project at this time. Keep interaction and visual choices simple and readable as a baseline quality expectation.
